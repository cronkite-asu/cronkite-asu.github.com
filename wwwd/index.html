Emergencies

Q: How should I go about interviewing an officer about an incident?

A: Often, a Public Information Officer (PIO) will be at the scene if a high amount of media attention is expected. PIOs often will be the only people to provide information at a scene. If you would like to speak with an officer instead of a PIO, visit the local police station for an interview or ask the PIO to set you up with someone to speak with.

Become familiar with the Sunshine Laws in your state to know what information a police officer is required to give up.

Viewing an arrest log can answer a lot of questions. Once you find the arrest that peaks your interest, request an arrest report to familiarize yourself with the situation, and THEN ask the police officers questions.


Q: How to talk to victims and family of victims?

A: When dealing with a victim, identify yourself as a reporter but be sure to establish a human connection by leaving all equipment behind. Know what you are going to say in order to avoid missteps, which can discourage the victim. Let the victim know that you are giving them the opportunity to tell their story.
